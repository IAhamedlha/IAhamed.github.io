Portia White's Career

Portia white was the first black Canadian concert singer to win internation acclaim (praised by more than one country.) She is known for one of the best classical singers. Her voice was desrcibed as a "gift from heaven."

She toured North America with Columbia concerts, South and Central America back. Later around 1946, she started experiencing vocal diffulties and also management problems. She toured Maritimes, Switzerland and in France. But then soon retired from doing public singing.

Later in 1952, she moved to Toronto and started teaching voice privately and at a school. She also resumed singing publicly for some concerts and officially ended public singing that took place in July 1967 at the World Baptist Federation in Ottawa.

White did not make any studio recordings but she had one song called Roses Never Fade.
