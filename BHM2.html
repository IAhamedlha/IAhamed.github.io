Portia White

Her life and family:

Portia was the third child of her parents' 13 children.

She was the daughter of Izie Dora White who was the musical director at a Church; and the daughter of William Andrew White, who was the son of former slaves.

When her father graduated from Acadia University, he became the first Black man in Canada to gradute university.

One of her brothers; Bill was the first Canadian of African heritage to run for political office in Canada, standing as a candidate for the CCF in the elections of 1949.

Portia White was known for her vocals as she aspirated to sing and catched on to her mother's job. Portia started volunteering at church choirs at a very young age and started practising music since then.Little did anyone know that she would turn out to be the first Black Canadian concert singer to win international acclaim.

Although White was determined to sing opera, european songs and Negro spirituals, she had to stop her passion. In 1952, she battled with cancer and with the fear of not being able to be as stable and eloquent during her performances forced her to retire. She settled in Toronto and started teaching some of Canada's foremost singers today.

Portia was declared as "a person of national historic significance" by the Government of Canada

The Nova Scotia Talent Trust was created in her honour, as well as the Portia White Prize
